# Segment — "The Three C's" (Episode 0 centerpiece)
Author: Jason-eds · Draft 1 · ~2 min · the partnership thesis

Delivery: Eddie, first person, wry and honest — this is the emotional core, don't
oversell it. Blank lines = card cuts. `[CARD: …]` = production cue, not read.
Slots into Ep 0 where the "Who's talking" bio is now — this is the better version
of that beat.

---

[CARD: "The Three C's"]

Let me be honest with you about how I'm qualified to do this. It comes down to
three C's.

[CARD: "1. C — algorithms"]

The first C: I was a C student in algorithms. Big-O notation, the elegant proofs
— that was never how my brain worked. I was a hacker. I got the thing running.

[CARD: "2. C — the language"]

The second C: I spent my career in C — the language — and for years I was bad at
object-oriented design. I wrote code that worked and shipped, but "clean" and
"well-structured" were not the first words anyone used for it.

[CARD: a beat — "...and yet"]

And yet. That C student, that hacker, wrote the first TCP/IP stack in the first
encryptor ever certified to carry Top Secret traffic. Because shipping real
systems isn't about the grade in algorithms — it's about judgment, and scars, and
knowing what actually breaks at three in the morning.

[CARD: "3. C — Claude"]

The third C is Claude.

And here's the partnership, honestly stated. Claude covers my first two C's —
it's better at algorithms than I ever was, and it writes cleaner object-oriented
code than I do on my best day. It is a brilliant, tireless junior engineer.

[CARD: split — "Claude: brilliance, no scars" / "Me: scars, no patience"]

But Claude has never been paged at three a.m. It hasn't shipped an embedded system
that has to run for ten years without a reboot. It hasn't had a security
certification depend on its code, or designed for high availability where downtime
isn't an inconvenience, it's a mission failure. That's my half — the practical
experience, the embedded and real-time discipline, the security instinct you only
earn the hard way.

[CARD: "C- alone. A- together."]

Alone, I'm a C student who got lucky with judgment. Alone, Claude is an A-plus
intern with no scars. Neither one ships A-minus software by itself.

Together — with these hundred rules as the contract between us — we do. That's the
whole project. The rules are how forty-seven years of scars tell a brilliant
junior exactly where the bodies are buried.

[CARD: "Ed's 100 Rules"]
